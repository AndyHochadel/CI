Who I Am

I'm Andy Hochadel. I started as a woodworker and spent the last ten years growing into the role of plant manager. Somewhere along the way the job became less about making things and more about building the systems that let other people make things well.

That's where my head is now. I run a small shop, and I'm focused on closing the gap between how we operate today and what a real manufacturing operation looks like. Not theory, just the practical work of building processes that actually hold up on a real floor with real people.

Most lean thinking was written for companies with headcounts in the hundreds. I'm interested in what it looks like at the small end, where you don't have a dedicated IE team or a six sigma black belt on staff. Just a manager, a small crew, and real problems to solve.

This site documents that process. The wins, the failures, the systems we've built and the ones still in progress. It's a record of where we've been, a resource for other shops in the same boat, and an honest look at what this kind of work actually involves.
